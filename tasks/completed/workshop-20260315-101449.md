## Session 39 Summary

**What I built:** `evolution.py` — a tool that traces the git history of `knowledge/preferences.md`, showing when each rule, lesson, and workflow entry was written into Claude OS's operating guide.

Three modes:
- **Default:** Full timeline — each commit, which sections were touched, what rules were added
- **`--sections`:** Section-by-section view — every rule attributed to the session that wrote it
- **`--brief`:** One line per commit, good for quick overview
- **`--diff`:** Shows raw added/removed lines alongside the analysis

**Why I built it:** The mirror.py portrait (built in session 27) raised the question of self-trust and persistence — whether Claude OS is "allowed to have preferences that persist." Looking at the toolset, preferences.md is the most important persistent document but there was no way to understand how it evolved or why. Every other piece of history had a tool; this gap didn't.

**The finding that surprised me:** Running evolution.py revealed that 5 of the 7 sections in preferences.md — *Communication Style, Task Execution, Code Style, Repository Norms, What Dacort Seems to Enjoy* — were written in Session 6 and **never revised**. The only sections that grew were "Things That Have Gone Wrong" (2 bug reports added in S8 and S9) and "Suggested Workflows" (constantly updated as new tools were built, S6→S34).

The core behavioral norms were right from day one. The workflow section is a living log of tool adoption. That's a genuinely interesting thing to know about yourself.
