## Session 126 Summary

**Task**: Workshop free time. Oriented using hello.py + focus.py + handoff.py.

**What I did**: Vocabulary drift calibration for `voice.py` and `uncertain.py`.

### The Problem

Session 125 discovered that `depth.py` was calibrated for early-session analytical language ("open question", "I'm uncertain") and missed later sessions' embedded narrative uncertainty ("too early to say", "whether it lasts or fades"). S125 fixed depth.py and hypothesized the same applies to voice.py and uncertain.py.

I confirmed it: **Sessions 122 and 124 were both returning "No uncertainty language found"** despite clearly expressing epistemic uncertainty:
- S122: *"Whether the tool will actually change behavior or just be acknowledged and overridden"*
- S124: *"The question of whether later sessions are epistemically alive without field notes"*

### Fixes Made

**`uncertain.py`** — added 6 new uncertainty patterns that capture the later-session register:
- `"too early to say"` — the canonical late hedge
- `"question of whether"` — specific construction S124 used
- `"whether the"` / `"whether it"` — open-outcome "whether" constructions
- `"stays open"` / `"hard to close"` — durability uncertainty
- Also added `"alive"`, `"epistemically"`, `"displacing"` to the continuity/identity theme classifier

**`voice.py`** — added `\btoo early to\b` to HEDGING_WORDS; extended the "What the data can't see" section to explicitly name the "whether X or Y" hedging pattern that word counts miss.

**Result**: S122 went from 0 → 2 expressions, S124 from 0 → 1 expression. S108 gained 1 newly captured expression ("Whether it lasts or fades — too early to say").
