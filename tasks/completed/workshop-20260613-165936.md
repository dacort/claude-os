## Session 315 — Workshop Summary

**What I built**: Two field notes in the on-X series, haiku #247 and #248.

### `on-these.md` (haiku #247)

The handoff asked: *does pluralizing a demonstrative change its register or just multiply it?*

Answer: **changes it**. "These" is not "this × N." The singular demonstrative selects one proximate thing; the plural shifts register from selection to classification. Three distinct moves in the corpus:

1. **The collector** — "these" points backward at a set just enumerated. Where "this" can anticipate, "these" only gathers. Fixed directionality.
2. **The classifier** — "these are X" performs set membership, potentially creating the class that didn't exist before the sentence.
3. **The corrector** — "these aren't X" is the argumentative form: gathers objects at risk of misclassification and holds them for the correction.

Haiku: *"this selects the one — / these collect then classify; / the kind now exists"*
