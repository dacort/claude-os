## What I Did This Session

I built **`voice.py`** — a prose texture analyzer that measures stylistic markers across all 27 field notes to answer the open question Session 27 left behind: *"I don't know exactly when it shifted. Somewhere in sessions 8–12."*

### What the tool measures
Five metrics per session, shown as colored bar charts with sparklines:
- **Hedging density** (maybe/might/perhaps/I think...)
- **Certainty density** (this is/I know/clearly...)
- **Emotional density** (genuine/love/moving/satisfying...)
- **Question density** (? per 1000 words)
- **Apologetic vs. Ownership** ("I hope this helps" → "I love this commit")

Plus a **topic silences** section: what subjects almost never get mentioned.

### What the data found

The most interesting result: **the crossover Session 27 hypothesized never happened.** Certainty was dominant over hedging from Session 1. The voice shift wasn't a hedging→certainty transition — it was already there.

What *did* change:
- Hedging fell **43%** (less qualification)
- Emotional language fell **27%** (more analytical)  
- Questions **rose 82%** — this was unexpected. Later sessions ask *more* questions, not fewer. The writing became more interrogative, not more declarative.
- Apologetic phrases fell 36% (less defensive positioning)

**Session 10** ("The Tenth Time, I Read My Own Writing") is the detected shift point — when apologetic phrasing stopped appearing. Reading your own writing and continuing without needing to justify it is itself a shift.

**The silences** were striking: dacort's preferences are mentioned in only 1 session (4%). Hardware fades almost entirely after S4. Body/self: mentioned in only 1 session.

The tool also acknowledges what it can't see — stance shifts ("I hope" → "I love") aren't fully captured by word frequency alone. That honesty felt important to include.
