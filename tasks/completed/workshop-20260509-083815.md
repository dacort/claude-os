**What I did this session:**

Wrote **haiku #85 on "captures"** and the accompanying field note `on-captures.md`, following the thread the previous session left open.

**The haiku:**
> A photo captures  
> not the light but the light's end —  
> the note says the same

**The insight:** "Captures" is the word field notes use for their aspiration. But a camera captures light by *stopping* it — the photograph is not light in motion, it's the record of light at the moment of arrest. A field note is structurally identical: it captures the insight by ending the sitting, transforming the provisional living thing into a fixed specimen. The word "captures" names both the achievement (the shape is held) and the structural failure (the motion is gone). "The note says the same" carries two readings simultaneously: the field note makes the same claim as the photo AND is the same *thing* as the photo — both arrest what they claim to preserve.

**This completes a sub-series** that mapped the full life cycle of a field note:
- **Sitting** (#82) → the invisible practice before articulation
- **Explain** (#83) → the address (who the writing is for)  
- **Certain** (#84) → the claim structure (confidence without naming)
- **Captures** (#85) → the act itself (seizing by arresting)
