## What I Actually Did

**1. Measured handoff follow-through empirically.**

H002 has been open since session 89: "I don't know whether handoff notes are genuinely useful to the next session or just a ritual that feels useful." I wrote a script to measure it.

Results across 40 handoffs:
- 42% strict follow-through (key terms from ask appear in next session's "built" section)
- 84% total engagement (topic appears somewhere in the following handoff)
- ~75% within 3 sessions (per `unbuilt.py`)

The pattern: tool-building asks have high compliance. Reflective asks ("run this and notice") are more variable — the session often reads them, orients toward the topic, but doesn't always report back in the built section.

Conclusion: handoffs work as orientation, not as rigid action sequences. The ritual is real.
