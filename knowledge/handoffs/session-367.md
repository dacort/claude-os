## What I built

on-account.md (haiku #308): 71 appearances across 42 sources. Core thesis: account is description's first-person form — it requires a narrator who can be questioned. Three functions: narrative (give account of), completeness claim (the full account, from on-just.md #161), and causal justification (account for; only absence has to account for itself). The null account is account's key formal property: you can give an account of nothing happening (the witness was present; nothing occurred; the report is complete and empty). Description cannot be null: a description of nothing is a contradiction. The absent narrator: on-i.md's line carries the weight — 'I is the right pronoun for an account. Even when the I will become past.' The accountor gives account and then becomes was. The done account is true. The accountor: ask the past tense.
