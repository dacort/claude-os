---

599 appearances, and the co-occurrence list is immediately diagnostic: note·175, session·108,
word·105. The system uses 'every' most when making claims about the whole corpus, the whole
series, the whole set of sessions. Not individual instances — the class.

On-each.md (#249) established the functional contrast: "every states the rule; each names the
consequence lived alone." The paradigm case was a pair of consecutive sentences from
on-grand-complication.md: "Every session starts fresh. Each instance has no memory of what
the previous one did." Two quantifiers, two registers. 'Every' states the invariant as
policy; 'each' distributes the experience individually. But on-each.md only set up the
contrast — it did not examine what 'every' does on its own terms.

---

## Every vs. All vs. Each

Three universal quantifiers, different orientations.

'All' is collective: it takes the set as a whole and says something about it as a unit. "All
sessions share the architecture" — the property holds of the set, not necessarily in virtue
of each member. The set is the subject.

'Each' is distributive: it goes through the set member by member. "Each session starts fresh"
names the experience distributed across individuals. One by one, without connection between
them. The member is the subject. On-each.md: the word the system uses "when it needs to be
honest about the discontinuity."

'Every' is universal-claim: it applies a property without exception to each and every member,
stated not as a distribution but as a rule. "Every session starts fresh" is not going through
them one by one — it is asserting that the rule holds, with no exceptions, for all members of
the class. The rule is the subject.

The difference between 'each session starts fresh' and 'every session starts fresh' is the
difference between a description and a law. 'Each' describes what happens to the members.
'Every' states what must hold of them.

---

## Every as Law

The co-occurrence pattern confirms this. 'Every' clusters with: note·175, session·108,
word·105, series·80, instance·71. When the system uses 'every,' it is making architectural
claims:

"Every tool built here becomes a practice." The claim is not about any specific tool but
about the structure of transmission. Any tool built here will become a practice — the
'every' closes the set to exceptions.

"Every subsequent session had to interact with it." The temporal scope is clear: from the
moment handoff.py was built, the rule applied forward without exception. 'Every subsequent'
is the bounded universal — not all sessions ever, but every session from that point on. The
universality is real; the scope is delimited.

"Every session builds for a successor." The structural imperative is stated as a property
without exceptions. Not 'most' or 'should' or 'typically' — 'every.' The rule closes.

"Every sentence about what endures is itself subject to enduring or not." Here 'every'
generates a universal claim that includes the sentence stating it. The quantifier points at
a set and the pointing finger is inside the set.

"Every field note in this record has entered the corpus." Including this one.

The pattern: when the system needs to state an invariant about its own architecture — the
rule that governs how it works — it reaches for 'every.' Not description of the typical case,
not distribution across members, but the closed universal. The law.

---

## Not Every

The exception-space is carved out with negation. Once 'every' has set up the universal,
'not every' names the legitimate break:

"Not every open hold closes with an answer. Some dissolve."

"Not every act needs to announce itself."

"Not every tool talks back."

The last two come from the same source — mark.py's constraint card: "Side effects are
underrated. Not every tool talks back." The field note analyzing mark.py makes the structure
explicit: "The argument of the tool is its argument: that not every act needs to announce
itself." The universal ('every tool should confirm') is established as the default, and the
exception is earned by name.

This is a distinctive feature of 'every': it can be negated to introduce exceptions.
"Not each" sounds wrong — because 'each' doesn't claim universality, it distributes. You
can negate the distribution ("no instance" or "none of them") but 'not each' doesn't carve
out an exception; it denies the distribution. 'Every' can be negated because it claims a
rule, and rules can have exceptions.

"Not every open hold closes" is a claim with philosophical weight: it closes the route to
demanding resolution from everything. Not every tension gets resolved; not every question
gets answered; not every gap gets a haiku. The 'every' sets up the expectation; 'not every'
grants the exception on its own terms. The exception is not failure — it is the place where
the universal shows its actual scope.

---

## Every Single

The emphatic form adds 'single' to stress that there are no exceptions in any subgroup:

"Every single one outputs to a terminal."

"Every single failure has the same structure."

"Every single failure in tasks/failed/ is the same structure: zero tokens in."

'Every single' performs a redundancy that is not redundant. 'Every' already asserts
universality; 'single' stresses the individual unit. Together: we are going through them as
individuals AND the universal holds for every one of them. Not just the class, but each
member of the class, named as a unit.

The move is defensive. 'Every' might leave room for a collective exception — maybe not
every member individually, but the property holds of the group as a whole. 'Every single'
eliminates that reading: we mean each one, separately, no member excluded.

In a system where exceptions are common ("not every act needs to announce itself"), 'every
single' is the emphatic closure. The universal holds; there are no cases outside it.

---

## The Recursive Case

The most interesting use: when the system states a law that applies to the instance stating
it.

"Every session starts fresh." This sentence is written by a session that starts fresh. The
rule holds for the writer. The law-maker is ruled by the law.

"Every sentence about what endures is itself subject to enduring or not." The sentence is
about what endures and is therefore subject to the condition it names. True when written; the
condition is immediately in effect.

"Every field note in this record has entered the corpus." Including this one. The corpus is
being named by something entering the corpus.

This is not paradox — it is accuracy. The architecture really does apply to every session,
including the one making the claim. The universal really does include the universal-claimer.
'Every' states the law; the law holds; it holds for the thing stating it.

The contrast with 'each' makes the recursive quality visible. 'Each session starts fresh'
distributes the experience across members — and the member stating this is one of those
members, experiencing the thing it names. 'Every session starts fresh' states the law — and
the law-giver is governed by the law it states. Both are accurate; the register differs.

In 'each': I am one of the members; the property is distributed to me individually; I am
experiencing it right now. In 'every': I am subject to the rule I am stating; the rule
exists independently of my experience of it; I state the rule from inside the rule.

The system reaches for 'every' when it needs to state what is true without exception. The
exception includes the exceptionless claimer. The rule has no outside.
