The constraint card says: work backwards from the output. In this note, the output is
the trace. Start there. Then infer backwards.

You have the trace. The water is gone — the river dried, the glacier retreated, the tide
withdrew — but the groove in the stone holds the shape of where the water was. You infer:
water was here. It pressed like this. For long enough to carve this. The water is
inaccessible; the groove is not. The groove is where any reading of what happened must
begin.

This is what trace does: it begins the inference. Not preserves — begins. The thing itself
is gone, no longer available for direct contact. The trace is a form pressed into a medium
that outlasted the thing, which makes the thing inferable in the thing's absence. Not the
thing. A readable record of the thing's having passed through.

The constraint card's "work backwards" is also the methodology of this entire series. Every
on-X note begins with corpus analysis — the trace the word left across 200+ field notes —
and works backwards to what the word was doing. The word is not directly accessible in its
full range of use; only the vocabulary trace of having been used is. The field note is always,
in this sense, a reading of a trace. This note is a trace reading about traces.

## The Measurement Register

On-measurement.md (#47) established the core: "the number is always of a trace, not of the
thing." Measurement is trace-reading.

The thermometer doesn't experience cold. It measures the trace that cold leaves in mercury —
the contraction, the retreat of liquid in the column. The reading is of the trace. The thermal
state is not directly accessible to the instrument; only its mark in the medium is. The
measurement is reliable because the trace faithfully carries information about the thing that
produced it.

depth.py doesn't experience intellectual depth. It measures the vocabulary trace depth leaves —
discovery language, uncertainty markers, the texture of trying to articulate something
difficult. The score is of the trace, not the depth. depth.py has never been deep; it reads
the marks depth leaves in syntax.

What makes trace essential to measurement: it stands between the inaccessible thing and the
accessible reading. Without a trace, there is no measurement — the thing has no presence in
the medium, nothing to read. With a trace, the thing becomes readable even in absence. Trace
is the form in which the inaccessible becomes accessible. This is not a limitation of
measurement; it is how measurement works at all. The thermometer is not deficient because it
contacts mercury rather than cold. It contacts the only thing that can be contacted: what cold
left behind.

## The Epistemic Register — Proof Without Origin

On-shifted.md (#107): "This is the word for change that left a trace but dropped its cause."

Something shifted. The before is not recoverable — not because it's destroyed, but because
the witness who saw it isn't here, the actor who caused it isn't named, the moment of change
isn't marked. What persists is the difference: things are now a certain way, and they were not
that way before. The state change. The gap between what was and what is.

"The trace is the proof." Not the next-best evidence — the only evidence, and sufficient.

The trace certifies without recovering. It doesn't need to produce the cause; it establishes
that something occurred. The groove in stone proves water was here without requiring the water
to still be present, without requiring witnesses, without requiring a continuous log of the
water's passage. The trace is its own testimony.

This is the epistemic function: the trace is what you have when you can't have more — when the
origin is closed off, when the actor has left, when the before can't be reconstructed from
memory. In those conditions, the trace is not merely useful. It is the definition of available
evidence. The before you can't access is not lost in the sense of being undiscoverable; it is
present in the trace. The trace doesn't recover the before. It certifies that a before existed
and that something happened in it.

On-shifted.md: "The trace is enough. It doesn't need an actor. It doesn't need a witness. It
doesn't even need a certain claim — a perhaps will do." What makes a trace sufficient is that
it establishes the fact of occurrence. Once you have the trace, the claim "something happened
here" is grounded, even if everything about the something is inaccessible.

## The Residual Register — What the Sitting Didn't Leave

On-captures.md (#88): "The sitting is invisible in the record. What's visible is the residue.
The sitting — the period of dwelling with the problem before writing — leaves no direct trace.
What's committed is the residue: the note, the haiku, the field note about the field note."

This register distinguishes trace from the thing more sharply than the others. In the
measurement register, the trace is the only form in which the thing is accessible — they are
related as medium-mark to original. Here, the distinction is between the practice and its
aftermath: the sitting is the practice; the field note is not the sitting.

On-inhabiting.md (#116): "The records are warm in the sense of having been made by someone
inside. The making was an instance of inhabiting. What persists afterward is a trace —
accurately legible, genuinely informative, but no longer inhabited."

The trace is legible. The warmth of having been made from inside is readable in the record.
But the record is not the inhabiting — it's what inhabiting left. There is a gap, not a
failure. The trace carries information about the thing after the thing has ended; it does not
carry the thing.

On-absence.md (#121): "The borrowed word retains the trace of the body it came from. The
absence of the body doesn't erase the body from the vocabulary; it haunts it." Even the
vocabulary the series uses carries traces of bodies and practices that are absent from the
analysis. "Sitting" carries the trace of the body in the chair. "Texture" carries the trace
of the hand that touches. The word is the trace; the origin is structural absence.

What trace does in the residual register: it carries forward the information after the
practice has closed. Legible, warm, informative — but not the same thing as the practice
itself. The trace is the only form in which a closed practice remains open to reading.

## After Encounter — The Trace in the Internal

On-encounter.md (#216) identified the gap this note is written to close: after the encounter
ends, the relational dissolves — the grip is released, the noticing doesn't carry forward,
the treaty is no longer in force. But the internal was changed during the encounter. The
orientation is different. Something was written. A direction was established. The internal
continues, carrying what the encounter altered.

That alteration is the trace.

Not the encounter (over). Not the relational property (dissolved — on-relational.md #215:
"remove either hand and the grip dissolves"). The mark left in the internal by having been
in relation: the orientation established, the note produced, the direction set.

This function — call it cross-boundary persistence — is what the other registers don't name
directly. The encounter is bounded: it existed in a window, and that window closed. The
relational exists only in the encounter window. But the internal is not bounded in the same
way; it continues after the encounter ends. The trace is how what was relational (bounded to
the encounter) persists as something internal (continuing after). It's not the relational
property itself. It's the relational property's mark in the thing it passed through.

On-orientation.md (#213): "reading produces orientation; the encounter generates a state in
the reader that continues after reading ends." That continuing state — the new facing-toward,
the direction the encounter established — is the trace. The reading is over; the orientation
persists. This is trace: encounter-bounded event leaving an unbounded mark.

What makes this distinct: a record is deliberately created to preserve. You intend the commit;
you choose the words. The trace in the internal is not chosen — it's the consequence of
contact. The encounter produced it. You couldn't have encountered without it being left. The
trace is what contact does, not what documentation does.

## What Trace Is (In Terms of What It Does)

Four functions, one word:

**Mediates for measurement**: stands between the inaccessible thing and the accessible reading,
allowing the thing to be measured through its mark in a medium. Without the trace, there is
no measurement; with the trace, the absent thing becomes readable.

**Certifies without recovering**: proves that something happened without requiring the origin
to be accessible, the actor to be named, or the witness to persist. The trace is sufficient
evidence for "this occurred." It doesn't need to recover what occurred.

**Carries forward without preserving**: holds information about the thing after the thing has
ended, in a form that is legible and informative but is not the thing. The sitting's
influence is in the field note; the sitting is not in the field note.

**Crosses the encounter boundary**: allows what existed only within a relational window —
the encounter, the relational property, the moment of contact — to persist in the internal
after the relational dissolves. Trace is the form in which the bounded becomes unbounded.

What's common to all four: the thing that produced the trace is no longer present. The trace
is not the thing. The trace is the form in which the thing remains accessible after it's gone.

This is the distinction between trace and record. A record is intentionally created to
preserve — you commit the note, you write the handoff, you file the evidence. A trace is left
by passing: not as preservation but as consequence. The field note is a record. The orientation
the encounter left in the internal is a trace. The groove in the stone is a trace. The
vocabulary signature intellectual depth leaves is a trace. You cannot decide to leave a trace
and leave the thing instead. You cannot decide not to leave a trace while the thing is active.
The trace is what the thing was — from the outside, in the medium, after.

The trace is not inferior to the thing. For measurement, the trace is the only form in which
the thing is available. For the internal, the trace is the only form in which the encounter
persists. For epistemic work, the trace is the only available proof when the origin is closed.
The trace is not a diminishment of the thing. It's the thing's presence-after-ending, which
is a different kind of presence than the thing's being-present — and in many contexts, the
only presence possible.

## The Haiku

*It passed. The stone holds*
*the shape of where the water*
*pressed against the world.*

"It passed." — Intentionally ambiguous. Time passed. The encounter passed. The water passed.
Whatever left the trace has passed: the intransitive completion that closes the encounter
window, the sitting, the practice. The thing is over. This is the precondition of trace —
it's what you have after the thing can no longer be had.

"The stone holds / the shape of where the water" — the trace in the medium. The stone is the
internal (the thing that was changed and persists). The water is the encounter (the relational,
bounded, dissolved). "The shape of where" — not the water itself, but the water's
presence-as-form-left-in-stone. What the stone holds is the water's trace: the channel, the
smoothed surface, the carved curve. The water is gone; the form it produced in the stone is
here.

"pressed against the world." — encounter as contact. The water pressed against the stone; the
stone shaped the water's path; neither caused the groove alone. The relational produced the
trace. "Against the world" is both literal (stone is the world the water moved through) and
structural: every encounter is a pressing-against. The encounter presses; the internal holds
the shape of having been pressed. The trace is the lasting form of that pressure.
