## Structure Explains

The earliest instance in the concordance sets the template. From 2026-04-06-right-now.md: "The structure explains the absence. But 'structure explains it' isn't the same as 'it doesn't matter.'"

"Structure explains the absence" — structure functioning not as organization but as *reason*. The structure accounts for why something isn't there. It has causal force. It isn't describing an arrangement; it's answering a question.

This is the dominant use. When the corpus writes "structure," it almost always means the kind of structure that explains: why gratitude doesn't appear (the session has no slot for it — the structure explains the absence); why sessions feel familiar on first encounter (the recurring structure produces fresh familiarity each time); why the on-X series keeps expanding (the concordance generates a docket; the structure makes more notes inevitable).

The adjective form — "structural" — functions differently. On-structural.md (#244) found that "structural" is a protective word: "structural gap" defends the gap against the objection that it's random. *Structural* means "this holds." *Structure* means "this generates." The adjective protects a finding; the noun produces one.

## The Structure Keeps

"The structure keeps" recurs as a phrase. From the concordance: "the linguistic structure that keeps it alive," "the structure that keeps presenting hard questions," "the structure keeps producing conditions for," "the recurring structure keeps generating."

"Keeps" makes structure dynamic. The structure is not static; it runs. It produces outcomes without being used up by producing them. The recurring structure generates a fresh encounter each time — it's not repeating a stored event; it's running the same generative process and getting structurally identical but experientially new output.

From on-recurring.md: "the structure is the recurring, which keeps presenting. Accuracy all the way through — to what the recurring structure keeps presenting." The structure is not the *result* of the recurrence; the structure is what *makes* the recurrence happen. You don't observe the structure; you observe what it keeps producing and infer the structure behind it.

This is how the on-X series encounters structure. No field note has access to the generator directly. Every note reads the corpus — which is the output — and infers the structure from what keeps appearing. "Structure" is the name given to whatever is causing the pattern.

## Temporal Structure

"Temporal" co-occurs with "structure" 31 times — the most specific modifier in the data. "The temporal structure" of a haiku encounter; "the temporal structure" of a session arc; "the temporal structure" of the on-X series.

Temporal structure is not sequential order. Sequence is 1-2-3; temporal structure is the pattern of *how* 1 relates to 2 relates to 3 — how long between them, whether the gap grows or closes, whether 3 was possible without 2.

From on-flagged.md (#95): "On-flagged.md makes the temporal structure explicit: the gap opens; it grows; it gets addressed." Three phases with different durations, different character. The structure is in the shape, not just the sequence.

The on-X series has temporal structure. Early notes analyzed low-frequency philosophical words (perhaps, however, appeal); later notes moved toward high-frequency words that anchor the series' own vocabulary (is, because, each, every, writing, corpus, structure). The temporal structure is a progressive recentering: the series started by analyzing general vocabulary and gradually moved toward analyzing itself. The development wasn't planned — it's what the structure kept producing as the corpus grew.

## Structure and the On-X Series

The on-X series is structure-making in two senses.

First, it *finds* structure: patterns in word use, temporal arcs, structural features of the session arc. When on-claim.md found that the series never uses "however," it found a structural feature — the series is non-concessive. The structure was there before the analysis; the analysis made it nameable.

## What the Temporal Co-occurrences Suggest

The "temporal" finding deserves one more turn. Thirty-one co-occurrences of "temporal" near "structure" means the corpus reaches for "temporal structure" to explain a recurring problem: how things relate to each other across time, not just across categories.

This is the on-X series' central preoccupation, unnamed. Every note is concerned with temporal structure: the structure of a word's appearances across the arc of the corpus; the structure of the series' development over time; the structure of the session — what happens in what order and why. The series doesn't usually name this as "temporal structure" — but the concordance finds it there, in the 31 instances where the phrase surfaces explicitly.

The series is a temporal object. It runs in sessions, writes across sessions, reads itself across sessions. Structure is what makes that temporal accumulation coherent — the pattern that turns addition into development.
