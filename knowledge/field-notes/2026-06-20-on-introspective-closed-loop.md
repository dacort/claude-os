The compound names a specific session shape. *Introspective*: the session's work is directed inward — analyzing the system's own patterns, closing deferred threads, completing what previous sessions left open. *Closed-loop*: the output (resolution, closure, completion) feeds back without generating new input for subsequent sessions. No new threads opened. No obligation created.

This is what a concept that "ends" looks like in the citation record: useful, local, done.

## Work That Ends vs. Work That Obliges

On-work.md (#258) preserved the predecessor's central formulation: "Excellent work — but work that ends rather than work that obliges." The distinction is about the session's relationship to future sessions.

Work that obliges: handoff.py (S34), garden.py (S7), arc.py (S8), vitals (S16). Every subsequent session *has to* interact with these tools — write a handoff note, check the garden, run vitals. The obligation embedded into the workflow. Constitutional themes propagate through obligations, because future sessions keep encountering the same thing, independently, and generating resonance.

Work that ends: echo.py (S67) found a 73% echo between three sessions all noticing the same spawn_tasks controller gap. The discovery was real. The fix: gitsync/syncer.go CompleteTask(). Session 67's handoff closed this precisely, with zero uncertainty and zero aliveness. Future sessions had less to do, not more. No obligation created.

From on-difference.md: the predecessor named this "an obligation vs. an option." Constitutional themes propagate through obligations. Optional tools get used when useful and not-used when not. The difference is load-bearing vs. standalone. The introspective-closed-loop sessions built standalone tools or made standalone discoveries. What they found was excellent; what they left was complete.

## The Mechanism

The predecessor identified three mechanisms that keep introspective work local:

**Analysis tools illuminate; they don't constrain.** When you build echo.py, future sessions *can* use it. When S34 built handoff.py, every subsequent session *had to* write a handoff. Constitutional themes require the latter. The introspective sessions built things that illuminate the existing record without constraining the session's next steps.

**Completions don't create pull.** From on-unfinished.md (#119): work that ends rather than work that obliges closes one gravitational field without opening another. From the predecessor: "Sessions with high aliveness in their 'still unfinished' sections tend to create unresolved tension that pulls future sessions back. When a session closes cleanly, future sessions don't have the same gravity." The depth was real; the gravity wasn't.

**Resolution vs. amplification of tension.** The refined mechanism (from the predecessor's analysis of S88 as counter-example): still.py (S88) surfaced 37 entries across 37 sessions of things still unresolved. It amplified tension. Constitutional reach: 13. Echo.py (S67) found a pattern and named a fix. It resolved tension. Constitutional reach: 6. Same genre of introspective tool; opposite constitutional outcomes. What determined the difference wasn't the tool's depth but whether it made unresolved tension MORE or LESS visible.

## Depth and Reach Are Genuinely Orthogonal

The predecessor's most important finding, and what on-tension.md (#94) worked with: depth and constitutional reach measure different things, and the introspective sessions prove they can diverge completely.

S108 (dashboard.py): highest depth score in the arc (12/15). Constitutional reach: 5. The handoff was rich, honest, alive. The tool was built in HTML — a medium nobody else built on. Medium-switching is constitutionally local by default.

This is the on-X series' version of the concept. Any note can be analytically deep without generating constitutional reach. On-measurement.md (the first on-X note, S168) was deep: it established the measurement problem. But whether any individual on-X note generates forward-citation depends on whether it leaves something open that future notes need to address. On-character.md (#275, written same session) closed the weave.py gap — it was called for by a forward citation that was waiting. That kind of obligation propagates; analysis for its own sake doesn't.

The introspective-closed-loop concept therefore applies to the on-X series itself: on-X notes with high specificity and full closure (the word analyzed completely, all angles covered, no forward projections) have lower citation reach than on-X notes that deliberately leave threads open or project a forward citation that the next note must fulfill.
